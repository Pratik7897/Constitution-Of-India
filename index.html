Fundamental Privacy Paradigm

Puttaswamy v. Union of India

Decided: August 24, 2017

Soham Zagare

Legal Technology Analyst

Procedural Timeline

2012

Justice K.S. Puttaswamy (Retd) challenges the constitutional validity of Aadhaar on privacy grounds.

JULY 2017

A 9-judge Constitution Bench is formed to decide if privacy is a fundamental right.

AUG 24, 2017

Unanimous verdict declared: Privacy is protected under Articles 14, 19, and 21.

1. The Compliance Standard

The proportionality test established in this case is now the gold standard for auditing state surveillance. It requires every intrusion to have a Legitimate Aim, a Rational Nexus, and Necessity.

2. Digital Sovereignty

In an era of big data, this judgment functions as a 'Digital Magna Carta'. It ensures that technology serves the person, and not the other way around. Every data-sharing protocol must now clear the hurdle of 'Limited Purpose'.

3. Impact on Legislation

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act of 2023 is a direct legislative descendant of the Puttaswamy mandate. It codifies the principles of consent, notice, and accountability into Indian law.

4. Conclusion

Puttaswamy ensures that our digital lives are shielded by the same constitutional armor that protects our physical persons. It is the living constitution adapting to the silicon age.

Verdict Snapshot

Bench Size

9 Judges

Decision Ratio

9:0 (Unanimous)

JUDICIAL SPLIT:
"Privacy is the ultimate expression of the individual's dignity."

Core Impact

  • ★ Data protection laws mandated
  • ★ Surveillance oversight required
  • ★ Decriminalization of consensual acts (Sec 377) enabled
The Basic Structure Doctrine

Kesavananda Bharati v. Kerala

Decided: April 24, 1973

Verdict Snapshot

Bench Size

13 Judges

Decision Ratio

7:6 (Split)

JUDICIAL SPLIT:
"Parliament may amend, but it cannot destroy the Constitution's soul."

Irreducible Values

  • ★ Supremacy of the Constitution
  • ★ Separation of Powers
  • ★ Secular & Democratic character

Nitya Gajarmal

Constitutional Writer

Historical Timeline

1970

Kesavananda Bharati challenges the Kerala Land Reforms Act seeking to protect temple lands.

OCT 1972

Largest Bench in SC history (13 judges) begins the marathon 68-day hearing.

APR 24, 1973

The 703-page judgment establishes the 'Basic Structure Doctrine'.

1. The Man Who Lost

Kesavananda Bharati lost his case. He never regained his properties. Yet, he achieved immortality by providing the judiciary with the shield it needed to protect democracy during the Emergency in 1975.

2. The Parliamentary Conflict

The core question was: Does Parliament have unlimited power to amend anything? The Court ruled that while 'Amending' is allowed, 'Rewriting' or 'Destroying' the foundational identity is forbidden.

3. The Global Legacy

The 'Basic Structure Doctrine' is India's greatest contribution to global constitutionalism. It has been cited by courts in Bangladesh, Kenya, and Belize to protect their own democracies from authoritarian shifts.

4. Final Reflection

The Constitution is not just a book for lawyers; it is the promise of a civilization. Kesavananda reminds us that the document's soul is permanent, even as its laws evolve.
